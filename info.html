Essential Cyber Safety Tips

Follow these simple tips to strengthen your online security.

Use Strong Passwords

Create unique passwords using letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid simple choices like "123456" or using the same password for multiple accounts.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

2FA adds an extra verification step (like a code from an app or SMS) so even if your password is stolen, attackers cannot access your account.

Avoid Phishing Scams

Phishing scams often come via email or SMS. Check the sender, look for spelling errors, and never click suspicious links or give personal info.

Common Cyber Threats

Learn about the most frequent dangers in cyberspace.

Phishing

Fake emails or messages trick users into revealing sensitive data. Always verify the sender and avoid clicking unknown links.

Malware

Malicious software that disrupts devices, steals data, or allows unauthorized access. Avoid downloading from unknown sources.

Ransomware

A type of malware that locks your files and demands payment. Regular backups and updates can help protect against it.

Social Engineering

Hackers manipulate people into sharing information. Be cautious with unexpected requests or urgent demands.

Cybersecurity Facts

Eye-opening statistics about the world of cyber threats.

Weak Passwords

Over 80% of data breaches are caused by weak or reused passwords.

Attackers exploit simple passwords with brute force or stolen credential databases.
Phishing Attacks

Phishing accounts for 90% of breaches.

Scammers impersonate trusted entities via email, SMS, or calls to steal information.
Two-Factor Authentication

Enabling 2FA prevents 99% of automated attacks.

Even if a password leaks, accounts stay secure with a second verification step.
Ransomware Costs

Ransomware costs businesses billions annually.

It encrypts critical files and demands payment; backups and updates are key defenses.
